Our Story

A grill by the Laconian Gulf.

Gythio was the port of Sparta. Ships left here carrying purple dye, olive oil and warriors; they came back tired and hungry. A thousand years later not much has changed — the boats are smaller, the harbour is prettier, and the hunger is the same. George cooks for that hunger.

Our pita is travixti — pulled by hand, fried in olive oil until it crackles. Our meat is cut in the kitchen, threaded on wooden skewers, and grilled over real charcoal. Our cheese is kaseri, aged and sharp. Put them together and you have the tripleta: the one bite Mani is known for.

We're a few tables on Tzanni Tzannetaki, the street that runs along the harbour. Pull up a chair, order the skewer you want, watch the smoke rise. That's it. That's the whole idea.

Σήμα Κατατεθέν · The Signature

The Tripleta.

three things, one perfect bite

You won't find it the same way outside Mani. A travixti pita, a slab of matured kaseri, and a fresh-off-the-grill skewer — folded together while everything is still hot. It's the sandwich Gythio gave Greece.

01

The Pita

Μανιάτικη Πίτα Τραβηχτή

Dough pulled thin by hand, slipped into hot oil, and fried until the edges blister. Crisp outside, pillowy inside. Nothing like the flat pita you know from the city.

02

The Cheese

Τυρί Κασέρι

A thick slice of aged kaseri, sharp and a little funky. It melts just enough against the hot pita to bind the whole thing together.

03

The Skewer

Καλαμάκι

Your choice: pork, chicken, kebab, veal, pancetta, sheep, or veal sausage. Cooked over charcoal, never gas, seasoned with nothing more than salt, pepper, and oregano.
